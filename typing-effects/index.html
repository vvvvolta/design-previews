Typing effects

Ten ways an AI agent can deliver text. Aim: Apple-meets-Saint-Laurent. Premium, restrained, with one tiny dopamine moment per reply. Pick one — it ships across the live widget on every embedded site.

01
Classic typewriter
Char-by-char reveal with a blinking caret. The default ChatGPT look — familiar and reliable.
02
Soft fade-in
Each character fades from 0% opacity. Calmer, less jolty than typewriter — feels considered.
03
Glow trail
Each new character glows purple, then settles into the base text color. Dopamine cue per char.
04
Apple shimmer
Single sweep of light passes through the line as it streams. Pure premium gloss, zero per-char animation cost.
05
Whisper rise
Characters drift up 6px and fade in. Subtle vertical motion gives the line a soft physical weight.
06
Markdown ink
Each character starts blurred and sharpens. Reads like ink coming into focus on the page.
07
Saint Laurent serif
Italic serif with letter-spacing collapsing from 0.4em → 0. Decadent, editorial, made for luxury brands.
08
Pulse dot
Plain text reveal with a heartbeat dot trailing the caret. Less typography choreography, more "thinking" presence.
09
Word burst
Whole words appear, not characters. Faster perceived delivery, less janky. Best for long replies.
10
Aperture
Single clip-path sweep wipes the entire line into view. Cinematic, single-frame premium reveal.
How this ships. Once you pick a winner I wire it into the live ChatManager so every embedded widget on every customer site uses it. The selection lives on AgentCustomization (per-agent), with a sensible default so existing widgets pick it up on next reload.
